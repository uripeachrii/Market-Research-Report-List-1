<p><p>The glucose market is primarily categorized into two types: D-Glucose Monohydrate and Anhydrous Glucose. D-Glucose Monohydrate, a crystalline form containing water, is commonly used in food and pharmaceutical applications due to its solubility and sweetness. Anhydrous Glucose, on the other hand, is a dehydrated form, offering concentrated sweetness and lower weight, making it ideal for energy products and specialized formulations. Both types serve distinct purposes across various industries, including food, beverages, healthcare, and nutrition.</p></p>
