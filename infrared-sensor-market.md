<p><p>The infrared sensor market is primarily divided into thermopile and pyroelectric infrared sensors. Thermopile infrared sensors detect temperature variations by measuring infrared radiation, commonly used in non-contact temperature measurement and industrial applications. In contrast, pyroelectric infrared sensors sense changes in infrared radiation emitted by moving objects, making them ideal for motion detection in security systems and consumer electronics. Both types cater to diverse applications, including automotive, healthcare, and automation, driven by growing demand for smart technology and effective sensing solutions.</p></p>
