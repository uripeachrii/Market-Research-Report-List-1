<p><ul><li>Single Core Type</li><li>Two Core Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phase-shifting transformers (PSTs) are categorized into single core and two core types, primarily based on their internal structure and functionality. Single core PSTs utilize one transformer core to adjust the phase angle of the power flow, making them compact and cost-effective for certain applications. Conversely, two core PSTs employ two separate cores, allowing for greater flexibility in managing power flows and enhanced voltage regulation, making them suitable for more complex grid requirements. Each type serves specific market needs efficiently.</p></p>
