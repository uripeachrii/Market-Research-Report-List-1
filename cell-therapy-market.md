<p><p>The cell therapy market can be divided into two primary types: stem cell and non-stem cell therapies. Stem cell therapies involve the use of undifferentiated cells capable of developing into various cell types, often used for regenerative medicine and treating conditions like blood disorders or neurodegenerative diseases. Non-stem cell therapies utilize mature cells or tissue-engineered constructs, focusing on specific cell types for targeted treatment, such as immune cells in cancer therapies. Both types aim to repair, replace, or regenerate damaged tissues.</p></p>
