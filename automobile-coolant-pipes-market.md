<p><p>The automobile coolant pipes market comprises three primary types based on material: metal, rubber, and plastic. Metal coolant pipes, often made from aluminum or stainless steel, are durable and resistant to high temperatures but may be heavier. Rubber coolant pipes offer flexibility and resistance to corrosion, making them suitable for various applications but with limited lifespan. Plastic coolant pipes, lightweight and cost-effective, resist corrosion and are often used in modern vehicles, though they can be less heat-resistant than metal alternatives.</p></p>
