<p><p>The intra-cranial pressure (ICP) monitor market is divided into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive monitors involve surgical placement of devices like ventriculostomy or intracranial bolt, providing accurate real-time pressure readings but carrying risks of infection and complications. Non-invasive monitors utilize techniques such as transcranial Doppler or infrared spectroscopy, offering safer alternatives with less precision. The choice between these types depends on patient condition, required accuracy, and potential risks, influencing market demand and technological advancements.</p></p>
