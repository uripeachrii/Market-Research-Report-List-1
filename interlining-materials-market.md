<p><p>Interlining materials are essential in the textile industry, primarily used to enhance fabric structure and durability. The market is divided into two main types: fusible interlining, which is coated with adhesive on one side and bonds to the garment fabric when heat is applied, providing a crisp finish; and non-fusible interlining, which relies on stitches or other methods for attachment, offering more flexibility and comfort. Each type is chosen based on the specific requirements of the garment.</p></p>
